Fairness Decision Authority Matrix

Use this matrix to identify who has authority to make different types of fairness decisions.

Decision Type Authority Example
Set organization-wide fairness vision Executive Council "We commit to equal opportunity across all hiring products"
Approve fairness budgets > $100K Executive Council "Allocate $250K for external fairness audits"
Launch high-risk product with known bias Executive Council "Approve hiring tool despite 7% disparity with mitigation plan"
Set maximum acceptable disparity thresholds Executive Council "No product may exceed 5% demographic parity difference"
Develop fairness metric selection guidelines AI Ethics Board "Use equal opportunity for hiring, demographic parity for lending"
Create cross-team fairness standards AI Ethics Board "All teams must conduct intersectional analysis for key groups"
Approve product fairness strategies AI Ethics Board "Resume screening may use reweighting + threshold optimization"
Resolve cross-team fairness conflicts AI Ethics Board "Hiring and background check teams must use consistent definitions"
Commission fairness audits AI Ethics Board "Conduct Q3 internal audit of all high-risk products"
Choose specific bias mitigation technique Product Team "Use adversarial debiasing instead of reweighting for this model"
Allocate sprint capacity to fairness tasks Product Team "Reserve 20% of next sprint for fairness testing"
Prioritize fairness technical debt Product Team "Address age bias before geographic bias next sprint"
Select test datasets and protocols Product Team "Use 500 samples per demographic group for validation"
Document fairness properties in model cards Product Team "Known limitation: May undervalue freelance experience"
⚠️ Note: Decision authority does not mean deciding alone. All decisions should involve consultation with relevant stakeholders. This matrix clarifies who has final authority and accountability for each decision type.
